Nafisha's Fav Books TOP 10

Ten Favorite Books

1.Jitterberg Perfume by Tom Robbins
"Okay, so thOkay, so this book has an 8th century time-traveling Bohemian king, a hyper-sexual goat-god, a New Orleans perfumer and a character actually called Bingo Pajama (among many, MANY other things). Need I say more?"
Jitterberg Perfume

2.One Hundred Years Of Solitude by Gabriel Garcia Marquez
"One of the best family sagas + magical realism books to have ever been written. It gets super confusing however. Seriously, there are 6 characters with the SAME name."
Garcia Marquez

3.The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle by Haruki Murakami
"This book is so big that could hit someone over the head with it and give them a concussion... But somehow I wish it were longer."
Haruki Murakami-trailer

4.Hard-Boiled Wonderland And The End Of The World by Haruki Murakami
"No one does surrealism like Murakami. If you can't tell already, he's my favorite author by a mile."
Haruki Murakami

5.To The Lighthouse by Virginia Woolf
"It was a hard book to get through since some sentences ended up being the length of an entire paragraph, but I learned to let myself just drift through Virginia Woolf's prose. No other book I've read has ever captured the human thought process so perfectly."
Woolf-summary

6. Sapiens: A Brief History Of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari
"Biology, anthropology, palaeontology, economics, evolution, history and all that good stuff all in one life-changing book."
Yuval Noah Harari

7. Radiance by Catherynne M. Valente
"Decopunk pulp sci-fi alt-history space opera mystery set in Hollywood. Yes."
Catherynne M. Valente

8. Cosmos by Carl Sagan
"The one book that should be mandatory to read for every human on this planet."
Carl Sagan

9. number9dream by David Mitchell
"It's a drug trip/dream sequence in book format."
David Mitchell

10. The Sparrow by Mary Doria Rusell
"This list wouldn't be complete without one of the best sci-fi books of all time."
Mary Doria Rusell
